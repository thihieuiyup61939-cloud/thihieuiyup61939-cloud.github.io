#### Low-Carbon Technology Support, Industrial Emissions, and Carbon-Price Feedbacks

**Independent research project | September 2025-present**

This study examines whether stronger support for industrial low-carbon technologies necessarily produces deeper cumulative emissions reductions under a common climate target.

- **Model:** Global Change Analysis Model (GCAM).
- **Scenario design:** technology-support start years of 2025, 2035, and 2045; multiple levels of low-carbon technology cost reduction; and comparisons across unconstrained, common CO2-constrained, and fixed-carbon-price scenarios.
- **Research outputs:** industrial CO2 pathways, endogenous carbon prices, cumulative emissions, energy-input substitution, sectoral responses, and regional mitigation burdens.
- **Preliminary finding:** technology support can reduce carbon-price pressure, but expansion of low-carbon inputs does not automatically lead to monotonically deeper cumulative emissions reductions. The simultaneous phase-out of high-carbon inputs is critical for stable mitigation outcomes.
- **Independent contribution:** research design, GCAM learning and configuration, scenario construction, model runs, database queries, output processing, visualization, interpretation, and manuscript preparation.
- **Current status:** working paper in progress.

#### Research Questions

1. How do the timing and intensity of technology support reshape industrial mitigation pathways?
2. Why can stronger support lower the endogenous carbon price without proportionally reducing cumulative industrial emissions?
3. How do low-carbon input expansion and high-carbon input phase-out jointly determine mitigation outcomes?
4. How do overlapping technology and carbon-pricing policies interact under a shared climate constraint?

#### Broader Research Agenda

I am interested in the links among formal climate ambition, policy implementation, technological change, sectoral transition, and policy overlap. My long-term research goal is to combine integrated assessment modeling with policy-relevant empirical and scenario analysis.
